         \n                <h2><a href="/series/big-data-childhood-infections-LMICs">Combating Childhood Infections in LMICs: evaluating the contribution of Big Data</a></h2>\n            \n\n            <div class="storyShortDesc">\n                \n                    <h4>The Lancet Infectious Diseases</h4>\n                \n                <h2 class="timeHeader">Published: December 13, 2021</h2>\n                <div class="content">\n                        <p>Communicable diseases such as sepsis, diarrhoeal diseases, and respiratory infections continue to be the leading causes of morbidity and mortality in children from low-income and middle-income countries (LMICs). Globally, the expansion of multi-omics and big data approaches have brought us unprecedented insights into host\xe2\x80\x93pathogen interactions in the context of infection and immunity. However, these advancements are often not translated into improved outcomes for children in resource-limited healthcare settings, such as the ones in LMICs.
